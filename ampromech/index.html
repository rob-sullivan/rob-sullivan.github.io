BIM Coordination

As modern building design evolves and adapts to our needs, additional building services lead to a more complex exercise in coordination. The common consensus is to resolve coordination issues at the design stage before they arise onsite, leading to a more cost effective and aesthetic final installation.

Building Information Modelling (BIM) processes and tools facilitate 3D visualization and representation of every building component and material for designers, manufacturers, and installation teams. The end result for the client is a data rich 3D model, fully representative of the installation physical properties, while also capable of storing as-built Operations and Maintenance (O&M) documents and data sheets, building material thermal and space environmental properties, and parametric interactions with the built environment.The BIM team at Ampromech pool several years of experience with BIM, spread across numerous construction industry sectors:

Educational facilities
Biopharma installations
Data Centres
Health Care facilities
Food & Beverages

The team has worked with industry BIM standards, and has been involved with several BIM level 2 projects in both the UK and in Ireland. The team is familiar with drafting and following execution plans, employers’ requirements, and task delivery plans to BIM standards and protocols. Staff are familiar and proficient with BIM software tools such as Revit, Navisworks, IES VE. These tools allow for Ampromech to design, coordinate, and simulate environmental conditions in a 3D environment in collaboration with all project stakeholders.

Power Generation

Power generation, as well as combined heat & power (CHP) projects are a particular area of expertise in Ampromech. We have direct hands on experience in all areas of power generation, ranging for combined cycle power plants with gas and steam turbines, as well as smaller simple cycle gas turbine power stations.

As well as power supply, we have 30 years experience in the specification and design of emergency power systems for critical applications using on-site diesel generation.

More recently we have been heavily involved in CHP (combine heat and power) projects wherein electrical power is generated withing a facility using natural gas as a fuel, and the heat generated during the combustion process is then harnessed and used to provide thermal energy for the facility in the form of steam and hotwater.

Arc Flash

We can carry out full arc flash assessments and reporting on existing and new facilities to ensure maximum personnel safety in switchrooms and near energised electrical equipment. We first build a model of the electrical infrastructure and look at all possible paths and magnitudes of power flow. Using SKM we can then generate a report identifying arc flash risks and boundaries and issue labels for all relevant equipment.

FOOD & BEVERAGE (PABC)

A 30,000m² existing distribution facility was redeveloped, at a cost of €XXm, by a leading US soft drinks company in Ireland. The team worked within a design build contract with one of Irelands leading construction firms, leading the design for electrical, mechanical and controls services and collaborating on the process systems services for the project.

The design facilitated both classified hazardous area and cleanroom design requirements and specifications. The provision of services for multiple process systems vendors was allowed for in collaboration with process systems designers.

Coordination of all services and construction materials was achieved through BIM processes and technologies. The scope of the project included for design, specification, tender analysis, and construction management.

MEDICAL DEVICES & BIOPHARMA

The team has been involved in several projects for a Medical Device Specialist in the eastern region of Ireland. These projects have included CHP design and construction management, service design and coordination in repurposed existing spaces, to the design of building extensions and integration with existing services and plant.

These projects cover a wide range of project activities from budget and cost analysis and projections, to tender and construction document preparation and management, and design of mechanical and electrical systems for each different area of the facility.

CINEMA LIFE SAFETY SYSTEMS

The team has been involved in the design of life safety systems for new and refurbished cinema units throughout Ireland over the past few years for a leading company in the entertainment industry in Ireland.

Construction management, site inspections and snagging, and BCAR certification were requirements for these projects.

HEALTHCARE FACILITIES

The team has been involved in several healthcare facility projects in Ireland and in the UK over the past decade. The facilities have ranged from primary care units, to Children’s hospitals, and multi-story critical treatment centres. Adherence to Health Technical Memoranda (HTM) is at all times crucial to the success delivery of these projects.

These facilities were design for Intensive and Critical Care Units, High Dependency Units, resuscitation bays, Cardiac Wards, etc. Isolated Power Supply units, critical and redundant supplies, onsite backup generation, and emergency evaluation lifts, to name but a few, were all common throughout these facilities, and had to be facilitated for in the mechanical and electrical designs.

EDUCATIONAL

The team has several years’ experience on working on first, second, and third level educational facilities in Ireland and the UK. These projects have ranged from existing school refurbishments and extensions, to new facilities on greenfield sites, and development of multi-story third level campus dorm buildings and science and law faulty installations.

Adherence to department of education guidelines and local building regulations was fundamental in all of these projects.
