About

Ampromech Consulting Ltd is a recently formed Irish Engineering Consultancy firm serving the Data centre, Pharma and Energy Sustainability sectors. Its directors, Fergus Cawley and Niall Flaherty, have combined over 50 years’ experience in the design and installation of industrial, pharmaceutical and power generation projects.  

Over the last 10 years they have been largely focused on datacentre design and have worked with several hyper-scale datacentre companies. Ampromech was established to offer more focused, niche and responsive solutions to best serve the schedules and value driven requirements of the customer.  

Ampromech have an experienced team of engineers on-board who have previously worked on multiple design projects over several years. The Ampromech philosophy is to utilise a strong core group of key people who are client focused and who understand the fast pace and realities of the current fast track project environment.

Our People

Fergus Cawley

Director

Fergus has a degree in electrical engineering from UCC, and has spent many years working on power generation projects as a start-up and commissioning engineer for Westinghouse Power Generation on multiple gas and steam turbine power plants all over the world, including the UK, Korea and South America. Subsequently Fergus worked for Pfizer in the pharmaceutical industry and then used his power generation experience to further the use of CHP (combined heat and power) installations in Ireland and in the UK. From 2010 Fergus has been involved in multiple DC projects, primarily in Ireland and Europe.

Niall Flaherty

Director

Niall has a degree in mechanical engineering and worked initially in the semi-conductor and pharmaceutical industries. He joined a design consultancy in 2007 and worked predominantly in the pharma and datacentre industry during that time. While there, he was lead mechanical designer on multiple datacentre developments and was also heavily involved in the master planning and MEP management for multiple different projects. Most recently he was MEP Project Manager for two datacentre buildings which were predominantly focused upon utilising a modular design for electrical plant rooms.
